Getting started

Keep it simple: a few tools, good soil, and the right plants for your space.

Essential tools

  • Trowel – for digging and transplanting
  • Bypass pruners – for clean cuts on stems
  • Watering can or hose – gentle flow is best
  • Gloves – protect hands, improve grip

Soil basics

Use a quality potting mix for containers and enrich garden beds with compost. Aim for consistent moisture—not soggy, not bone dry.

Tip: Mulch helps conserve water and suppress weeds.

Beginner-friendly plants

Choose plants that match your sunlight and climate. Try these reliable starters:

Cherry tomatoes

Prolific and forgiving; need 6–8 hours of sun.

Lettuce

Quick harvest; partial shade is fine in heat.

Herbs (basil, mint)

Great in containers; harvest often to encourage growth.
